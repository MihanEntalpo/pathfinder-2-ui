Primal energy exists for far more than being gathered, shaped, and directed by [druids](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=34.md); like air currents, it flows naturally through an environment. With the proper training, an explorer can perceive these currents, sensing cues carried on them like airborne scents. With further work, they can drift with these energies like a leaf on a river, or even hide themselves in the primal eddies to evade their foes’ senses. Too much exposure can erode the practitioner’s higher thoughts, leaving behind a predatory cunning that augments their combat abilities at the risk of wearing away their sense of self.

These techniques have developed independently, spread, and then faded to obscurity countless times, with the Verduran Forest boasting the largest population of practitioners, who are known as Verduran shadows. Developed by the hunter Treyinca centuries ago, Verduran shadow techniques are widespread among the region’s guardians but rarely taught to outsiders. Treyinca’s fate is unknown, and some speculate she transformed into a primal spirit that mentors woodland warriors to this day.

# Additional Feats

Source [Player Core pg. 215](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md)
Some archetypes include a list of “Additional Feats” that appear in other sources. The list includes each feat’s level, which might be different than normal when gained from the archetype. You can take the feat as an archetype feat of that level, meaning it counts toward the number of feats required by the archetype’s dedication feat. When selected this way, a feat that normally has a class’s trait (such as the fighter trait) doesn’t have that class trait.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Verduran Shadow Dedication Feat 2

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Wardens of Wildwood Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype Dedication
Source [Pathfinder #201: Pactbreaker pg. 79](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=241.md)
Archetype [Verduran Shadow](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=312.md)
Prerequisites trained in [Stealth](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=48.md) and [Survival](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=49.md)

---

Your awareness of primal energies allows you to explore forests with preternatural ease. You become an expert in [Survival](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=49.md). You can substitute your proficiency in Survival for your proficiency in [Stealth](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=48.md) when meeting feat prerequisites and gaining additional benefits from feats for being an expert, master, or legendary in Stealth; however, unless you also fulfill the Stealth prerequisite, you can only use those feats in forest terrain. While in forests, you can use your Survival modifier in place of your Stealth modifier when you Avoid Notice, [Hide](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2404.md), [Sneak](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2405.md), or would use Stealth to roll initiative.

Special You can’t select another dedication feat until you’ve gained two other feats from the Verduran Shadow archetype.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Canopy Predator Feat 4

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Wardens of Wildwood Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype Skill

---

You are as at home in the treetops as you are beneath their branches. You gain a climb Speed of 15 feet when moving through trees, vines, and other foliage. You aren’t [off-guard](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=58.md) while Climbing or Balancing on a tree.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Death from Above [two-actions] Feat 8

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Wardens of Wildwood Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype
Source [Pathfinder #201: Pactbreaker pg. 80](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=241.md)
Archetype [Verduran Shadow](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=312.md)
Prerequisites Canopy Predator; Verduran Shadow Dedication; expert in [Athletics](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=36.md)
Requirements You are standing, [climbing](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=347.md), or [balancing](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2369.md) on a surface at least 10 feet above your target.

---

You leap down on your target, using their body to cushion your fall and your momentum to empower your attack. Attempt an [Athletics](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=36.md) check to Leap to a space adjacent to the target. If you land adjacent to the target, compare the Athletics check result against your target’s Reflex DC.

When you leap onto a creature in this way, you do not also deal damage for falling on a creature. Regardless of your result, the target is temporarily [immune](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2312.md) to your Death from Above for 1 minute.

Critical Success The target falls and lands prone, reducing the falling damage you take by an amount equal to twice your level. You then can make a melee [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md) against the target. The Strike gains the [deadly d8](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=570.md) weapon property and deals an additional 1d6 bludgeoning damage. This additional damage increases to 2d6 if you are a master in Athletics, and to 3d6 if you are legendary in Athletics.
Success The target is [off-guard](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=58.md) until the beginning of your next turn, reducing the falling damage you take by an amount equal to your level. You can then make a melee Strike against the target, adding the deadly d8 weapon property to the attack.
Failure You can make a [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md) against the target.
Critical Failure You fall [prone](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=88.md), whether you take falling damage or not.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Sense the Strike [reaction] Feat 10

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Wardens of Wildwood Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype
Source [Pathfinder #201: Pactbreaker pg. 80](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=241.md)
Archetype [Verduran Shadow](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=312.md)
Prerequisites Verduran Shadow Dedication; master in [Survival](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=49.md)
Trigger A creature targets you with an [attack](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=540.md) and you can see the attacker.

---

You sense how an incoming attack moves through ambient primal energy, predicting how best to dodge. The triggering attack targets your [Survival](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=49.md) DC instead of your AC. Though this allows you to avoid taking penalties to your AC, it doesn’t remove any conditions or other effects causing such penalties. For example, an enemy with sneak attack would still deal extra damage to you for being [off-guard](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=58.md), even though you wouldn’t take the –2 circumstance penalty when defending against the attack.
