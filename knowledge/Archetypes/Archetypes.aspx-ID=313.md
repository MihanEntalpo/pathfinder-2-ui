# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)

Source [Pathfinder #207: Resurrection Flood pg. 80](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=249.md)
In Belkzen, an important part of a storyteller’s arsenal is body art—anything from depictions of past events to illustrate a legend to gruesome scars that prove the bearer faced real danger and survived. Belkzen [orcs](../Ancestry/Ancestries.aspx-ID=66.md) with extensive body art and scars are often dubbed historians for the stories they carry. The greatest of them can unlock these stories’ intrinsic magic to perform supernatural feats. A tattooed historian commands respect and reverence, often allowing them to travel safely among Belkzen’s holds.

This archetype represents the Belkzen version of a tattooed historian; other cultures might have developed variations of this tradition with different prerequisites.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Tattooed Historian Dedication Feat 2

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Triumph of the Tusk Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype Dedication
Source [Pathfinder #207: Resurrection Flood pg. 80](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=249.md)
Archetype [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)
Prerequisites Constitution +1; trained in [Orc Lore](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=41.md), [Orc Pantheon Lore](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=41.md), or [Belkzen Lore](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=41.md), or (at the GM's discretion) a related [Lore](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=41.md) skill

---

You become trained in [Diplomacy](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=39.md) or [Performance](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=45.md); if you are already trained in both skills, you instead become trained in a skill of your choice. You gain access to all uncommon magical tattoos with the [orc](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=666.md) trait.

You gain a storied skin for free upon gaining this feat (or another magical tattoo of 2nd level or lower if you already have storied skin). You cannot have more than one storied skin tattoo, but the frequency of its Living History ability increases by one use per minute for every three tattooed historian feats you have. For every two tattooed historian feats you have, you can invest one magical tattoo that does not count against the maximum number of items you can have invested at one time.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Agent of All Holds Feat 2

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Triumph of the Tusk Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype
Source [Pathfinder #207: Resurrection Flood pg. 81](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=249.md)
Archetype [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)
Prerequisites Tattooed Historian Dedication
Trigger You critically fail a [Diplomacy](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=39.md), [Intimidation](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=40.md), or [Performance](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=45.md) check.

---

Orcs across Belkzen recognize you as an honored lorekeeper. You’re adept at leveraging this status and showing off your tattoos to resolve conflicts, change minds, or distract others from a faux pas. You get a failure on the check, rather than a critical failure. If the triggering check was made against a creature with the [orc](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=666.md) trait, you can instead add the [fortune](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=612.md) trait to this ability and reroll the check, treating any critical failure as a failure.

Archetype [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)
Prerequisites Tattooed Historian Dedication
Trigger A creature targets you with an [attack](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=540.md), and you can see the attacker.

---

Your tattoos can briefly animate and extend from your skin, granting you a spectral shieldbearer who protects you from harm. Doing so expends one use of your storied skin, granting you a +1 circumstance bonus to AC against the triggering attack. In addition, you gain [resistance](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2312.md) to [mental](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=647.md), [spirit](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=737.md), and [void](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=510.md) damage equal to twice your number of tattooed historian feats against the triggering attack.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Infused with Belkzen's Might [two-actions] Feat 6

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Triumph of the Tusk Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype
Source [Pathfinder #207: Resurrection Flood pg. 81](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=249.md)
Archetype [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)
Prerequisites Tattooed Historian Dedication

---

Your tattoos immortalize the conquests of orc war leaders, such as Belkzen besieging the dwarven Sky Citadel of Koldukar. You can expend one use of your storied skin’s Living History ability to channel this power into your own weapons. Until the end of your next turn, you deal additional [spirit](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=737.md) damage with weapons and unarmed attacks equal to 1 plus half the number of [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md) feats you have.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Inscribed with Elders' Deeds Feat 6

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Triumph of the Tusk Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype
Source [Pathfinder #207: Resurrection Flood pg. 81](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=249.md)
Archetype [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)
Prerequisites Tattooed Historian Dedication

---

Your tattoos commemorate unique exploits that (according to legends) only an [orc](../Ancestry/Ancestries.aspx-ID=66.md) could accomplish. During your [daily preparations](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2575.md), you can reconfigure part of your storied skin to depict a specific orc hero, granting you a 1st-level ancestry feat with the [orc](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=666.md) trait until you prepare again; this ancestry feat cannot require any physiological feature you lack, as determined by the GM. Since this feat is temporary, you can’t use it as a prerequisite for permanent character options, such as for a feat. At 13th level, you can instead gain a 5th-level ancestry feat with the orc trait.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Wrath of the Hold [two-actions] Feat 8

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Triumph of the Tusk Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype Occult
Source [Pathfinder #207: Resurrection Flood pg. 81](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=249.md)
Archetype [Tattooed Historian](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=313.md)

---

Your tattoos carry the strength of an innumerable horde, and you can expend one use of your storied skin’s Living History ability to animate your tattoos as ghostly assailants that swarm your foes. These spirits attack all foes in a 30-foot cone, dealing 4d6 [spirit](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=737.md) damage. The damage increases by 1d6 at 10th level and every 2 levels thereafter. Each affected creature must attempt a basic Will saving throw against the higher of your class DC or spell DC.
