# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Ytildos (The Tidefang)](./Deities.aspx-ID=706.md)

Source [Pathfinder #216: The Acropolis Pyre pg. 76](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=271.md), [Myth-Speakers Players Guide pg. 11](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=270.md)
In an act of hypocrisy, Ongalte used her cyclopean powers to empower a lieutenant in her deicidal campaign: a human pirate named Ytildos. He served Ongalte for years before she was wounded in battle, at which point he stole her wealth and left her to die. Ever since, Ytildos has plied Kartaji Bay, always on the lookout for plunder. When he can't find treasure, he makes it, seeding misinformation about the treacherous straits and reefs to sink ships and their cargo. His followers are equally opportunistic. However, their patron is fickle and greedy, and when he senses a worshipper's windfall, Ytildos often swoops in to demand a cut.

Category Iblydos Hero-Gods
Edicts Capitalize on others' misfortune for profit, subtly ruin navigation tools, set traps
Anathema Despoil a coastal ecosystem, give away a treasure map for free
Areas of Concern Reefs and shipwrecks
Religious Symbol a ship wrecked atop a reef
Sacred Animal hermit crab
Sacred Color(s) brown, orange
