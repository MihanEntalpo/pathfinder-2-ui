# Undead Companions

Source [Book of the Dead pg. 37](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=118.md)
Undead companions are loyal, though not fully sapient, entities that follow your orders, whether as an undead animal or an undead humanoid rendered mindless or nearly so by undeath. They work like animal companions in most ways. Most undead companions are uncommon, typically only available to those with the [undead master](../Archetypes/Archetypes.aspx-ID=176.md) archetype or an intrinsic connection to the realm of the dead. The GM might determine a fallen animal companion can be animated as one of the following with the proper create undead ritual. Undead companions typically have slightly lower statistics than a normal animal companion to compensate for their immunities. You can have only one companion of any kind at a time.

### Legacy Content

Uncommon [Incorporeal](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=632.md) [Minion](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=653.md) [Undead](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=722.md)
Source [Book of the Dead pg. 38](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=118.md)
Your companion is a ghost, bound to an item in your possession. Due to this bond, your ghost companion can move beyond the area of a single dwelling, but it's easier to disrupt than most incorporeal creatures. A ghost companion can be formed from a humanoid, animal, or other creature. The ghost isn't sapient and is unable to speak with the living.

Senses darkvision
Speed fly 20 feet
Special anchored incorporeality
Support Benefit Your ghost spooks and scares your foes as you attack them. Until the start of your next turn, if you hit and deal damage to a creature in your ghost's reach, the creature becomes frightened 1.

### Telekinetic Assault [two-actions]

Divine [Evocation](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=65.md)
Source [Book of the Dead pg. 38](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=118.md)
Frequency once per hour

---

Your ghost unleashes a flurry of emotions, causing small objects and debris to fly about in a 20-foot emanation, dealing 1d6 bludgeoning damage for every 2 levels the ghost has (basic Reflex save). This uses a trained DC using the ghost's Charisma modifier or an expert DC if the ghost is specialized.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Gray Bladeling

Rare Construct [Minion](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=653.md) Undead
Source [Shining Kingdoms pg. 123](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=265.md)
Your companion is a vaguely humanoid construct composed of razor-sharp blades and animated by an amalgamation of restless soul fragments, similar to a miniature gray death. Although it possesses no identity or memories from the souls it contains, it’s driven by a powerful but unfocused hate that its master must sometimes struggle to control.
[PFS Note](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md) Characters from Galt have access to this option.

Access You're from Galt.
Size Small
Melee [one-action] blade ([agile](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=526.md), [finesse](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=602.md)), Damage 1d8 slashing
Str +2, Dex +1, Con +2, Int -4, Wis +0, Cha +1
Hit Points 10 (void healing)
Skill [Athletics](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=36.md)
Senses [darkvision](../MonsterAbility/MonsterAbilities.aspx-ID=59.md), lifesense (imprecise) 30 feet
Speed 20 feet
Special A gray bladeling has the [construct](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=563.md) and [undead](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=722.md) traits instead of the [animal](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=531.md) trait. It has [immunity](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2312.md) to [bleed](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=86.md), death effects, [disease](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=578.md), [doomed](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=67.md), [drained](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=68.md), [fatigued](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=73.md), [healing](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=623.md), [nonlethal attacks](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2311.md), [paralyzed](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=85.md), [poison](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=669.md), and [sickened](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=91.md). Unlike most undead, it becomes [unconscious](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=95.md) and [dying](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=69.md) rather than being destroyed instantly at 0 Hit Points; unlike most constructs, its Hit Points can be restored by only void healing instead of any other actions that restore Hit Points to objects or nonliving creatures.
Support Benefit The gray bladeling slashes viciously at your enemy when you create an opening. Until the start of your next turn, each time you hit a creature in the bladeling’s reach with a [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md), the creature takes 1d4 [persistent bleed damage](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=86.md) or becomes [frightened](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=76.md) 1 if it’s already taking persistent bleed damage. If the gray bladeling is specialized, the persistent bleed damage increases to 1d8.
Advanced Maneuver Possessing Strike

### Possessing Strike [two-actions]

Curse Mental Occult
Source [Shining Kingdoms pg. 123](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=265.md)
Frequency once per hour

---

Your gray bladeling attempts to inject a malevolent soul fragment into a victim to seize control of their actions. The gray bladeling makes a blade [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md) against a living creature. If it hits and deals damage, the target must attempt a Will saving throw against your class DC or spell DC, whichever is higher. Regardless of the result, the target is temporarily immune to Possessing Strike for 24 hours.

Critical Success The target is unaffected.
Success The target fights off the possessing soul fragment but is [stunned](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=93.md) 1.
Failure The target is [confused](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=63.md) for 1 round and can’t use offensive actions against you or your gray bladeling.
Critical Failure As failure, but if the target takes damage from an attack or spell, the DC of the [flat check](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=333.md) to recover from the confusion is 15.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Severed Head

Uncommon [Minion](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=653.md) Undead
Source [Shining Kingdoms pg. 123](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=265.md)
Your companion is an [undead](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=722.md) severed head. Most of the victims’ heads were unceremoniously dumped alongside their bodies, but many were snatched by unscrupulous necromancers who found them to be a convenient (and unfortunately plentiful) raw material for the creation of simple undead minions.
[PFS Note](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md) Characters from Galt have access to this option.

Size Small
Melee [one-action] jaws ([finesse](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=602.md)), Damage 1d6 piercing
Str +2, Dex +1, Con +3, Int -5, Wis +0, Cha +0
Hit Points 4 (void healing)
Skill none ([mindless](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=652.md))
Senses [darkvision](../MonsterAbility/MonsterAbilities.aspx-ID=59.md)
Speed 15 feet, fly 25 feet
Special A severed head has the [undead](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=722.md) trait instead of the [animal](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=531.md) trait, granting it void healing and immunity to death effects, [disease](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=578.md), and [poison](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=669.md). It’s also mindless.
Support Benefit The head latches on to your enemy with its jaws when you create an opening, distracting them and hampering their movement. The next creature within range of your severed head that you successfully [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md) before the start of your next turn takes a –5-foot status penalty to its Speeds and must succeed at a DC 5 [flat check](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=333.md) to use reactions triggered by your actions until the start of your next turn. On a failure, the reaction is lost.
Advanced Maneuver Gnash

### Gnash [one-action]

Source [Shining Kingdoms pg. 123](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=265.md)
Requirements The severed head’s last action was a successful jaws Strike.

---

Your severed head violently shakes itself back and forth, trying to tear away a mouthful of flesh. It makes another jaws [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md) against the same target. If the Strike hits, it deals 1d6 [persistent bleed damage](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=86.md) in addition to its usual damage.
