# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)

Source [Gatewalkers (Hardcover) pg. 240](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=290.md)
Centuries ago, Domora Hume founded a lakeside city that would become one of the largest in western Sarkoris. When the city was attacked by raiders, Hume conjured a guardian from the lake to drive back the attackers. In the process, he also become the region’s first god caller in generations.

Hume’s savior was an ancient spirit guide from the [Plane of Water](../Plane/Planes.aspx-ID=32.md) named Dyzad. With Dyzad’s guidance, Domorans summoned more spirit guides, all of which the citizens worshipped as gods long after his passing. This tradition of [god calling](../Deity/Deities.aspx-ID=200.md)—and the worship of [Sarkorian gods](../DeityCategories/DeityCategories.aspx-ID=15.md)—came to define the city of Domora.

In the ensuing years, countless pilgrims, Sarkorian or otherwise, came to Domora to learn of god calling. Many hoped to summon a god to bring back to their own people, while others petitioned the deities as a means of spiritual growth. Today, the Domoran god calling tradition is all but extinguished—wiped out during the Worldwound incursion. Nonetheless, worthy individuals who brave Domora’s ruins might unbury a fragment of this tradition. Although they aren’t true god callers—they lack the ability to bond with greater spirit guides or unlock the highest levels of their power— such “scions” still forge unique relationships with lesser spirit guides. Scions of Domora provide physical and spiritual anchors for their spirit guide companions, while the spirit guide returns the favor by sharing a portion of its supernatural powers.

### Scion of Domora (Archetype)

At the climax of “They Watched the Stars,” the party rescues a group of spirit guides. In so doing, Sakuachi proves worthy of a spirit guide—a Sarkorian god who vows to accompany her to the Crown of the World. Characters who accompany Sakuachi gain access to the following new archetype at the end of this adventure. This bond also changes the powers and nature of the spirit guide; the spirit guide becomes a [familiar](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=5024.md), and a PC with this archetype uses the spirit guide familiar statistics and abilities described in this archetype, rather than the normal benefits.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Scion of Domora Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8124.md) Feat 4

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Gatewalkers Adventure Path

Rare Archetype Dedication
Source [Gatewalkers (Hardcover) pg. 240](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=290.md)
Archetype [Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)
Prerequisites [Familiar Master Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=6328.md); you have befriended a spirit guide and it bonded with you using its Bond to Mortal ability.

---

You gain a spirit guide ally. Your familiar takes on the form of a spirit guide specific familiar.

Special You can take Scion of Domora Dedication even if you have the Familiar Master Dedication and have not taken two other Familiar Master feats. You can’t select another dedication feat until you have gained at least two other feats from the scion of Domora or familiar master archetypes.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Guided Skill](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8126.md) [one-action] Feat 6

Archetype

Archetype [Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)
Frequency once per day
Prerequisites [Scion of Domora Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8124.md)

---

You ask your spirit guide for aid. For the next 10 minutes, you gain a +2 status bonus to checks made using [Acrobatics](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=34.md), [Occultism](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=44.md), [Stealth](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=48.md), or [Survival](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=49.md).

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Spiritual Strike](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8125.md) [two-actions] Feat 6

Archetype Flourish
Source [Gatewalkers (Hardcover) pg. 240](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=290.md)
Archetype [Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)
Prerequisites [Scion of Domora Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8124.md)
Requirements You are wielding a weapon with at least one fundamental rune and your familiar is adjacent to you or in your space.

---

Your spirit guide enhances your attacks with occult power. Make a Strike with a weapon that has at least one fundamental rune. This counts as two attacks when calculating your multiple attack penalty. The Strike has the effects of the [ghost touch](../Equipment/Equipment.aspx-ID=2840.md) property rune and deals an additional 2d6 force damage. If your weapon has a [major striking](../Equipment/Equipment.aspx-ID=2861.md) rune, this Strike instead deals 3d6 force damage.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Guardian's Embrace](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8127.md) [one-action] Feat 8

Archetype
Source [Gatewalkers (Hardcover) pg. 241](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=290.md)
Archetype [Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)
Prerequisites [Scion of Domora Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8124.md)
Requirements You are adjacent to your spirit guide or it is sharing your space.

---

Your spirit guide discorporates to surround you in an invisible protective shroud. You gain a +2 status bonus to AC against physical attacks until the beginning of your next turn. If you are hit by a physical attack during this time, you can use a reaction to gain [resistance](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2312.md) 10 to physical damage dealt by that attack; doing so ends the AC bonus, and you can’t use Guardian’s Embrace again for 10 minutes. Your spirit guide cannot be targeted by effects, cannot be affected by area effects, and cannot take any actions while this ability is active. Your spirit guide appears in a space adjacent to you when the effect ends.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Spiritual Aid](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8130.md) Feat 12

Archetype
Source [Gatewalkers (Hardcover) pg. 241](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=290.md)
Archetype [Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)
Prerequisites [Scion of Domora Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8124.md)

---

When you call your spirit guide for aid, its presence bolsters your will and agility. You gain the Spiritual Aid reaction.

Spiritual Aid [reaction] Trigger You fail a Reflex or Will saving throw; Effect You reroll the triggering saving throw. You must use the second result, even if it’s worse. If you roll a success, you get a critical success instead.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Spirit Guide Form](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8131.md) [two-actions] Feat 14

Archetype Polymorph
Source [Gatewalkers (Hardcover) pg. 241](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=290.md)
Archetype [Scions of Domora](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=336.md)
Frequency once per day
Prerequisites [Scion of Domora Dedication](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=8124.md)

---

You fuse with your spirit guide, becoming a hybrid being with enhanced physical and spiritual power. This effect lasts 1 minute; you can end it early as a free action. During this time, you can’t use any other actions granted by this archetype. You gain the following statistics and abilities, regardless of your type of spirit guide.

- You become [incorporeal](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=632.md).
- You gain [resistance](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2312.md) 10 to all damage (except [force](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=610.md), [ghost touch](../Equipment/Equipment.aspx-ID=2840.md), or [spirit](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=737.md); double resistance vs. non-magical).
- Darkvision.
- An unarmed melee attack of the same type as your spirit guide (either claws or jaws), which is the only attack you can Strike with. You’re trained with this attack. Your attack modifier is +29 or your normal unarmed attack modifier, whichever is higher. You use the listed statistics for the unarmed attack type:
- Claws ([agile](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=526.md), [backstabber](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=544.md), [finesse](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=602.md)) [one-action] , Damage 3d6+5 slashing plus 1d6 force;
- Jaws ([forceful](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=611.md)) [one-action] Damage 3d10+5 piercing plus 1d6 force.
