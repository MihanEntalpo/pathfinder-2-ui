There is a visceral power in blood. It sustains life through its flow. Bloodragers exist throughout societies in different forms. Matanji [orcs](../Ancestry/Ancestries.aspx-ID=66.md) are [demon](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=573.md) hunters that turn the power of fiendish blood against its vessels. The Skoan-Quah protect the tombs of their dead, preserved with containers of their fallen companions’ blood. This blood is used by their closest living comrades in combat to utilize lost power and knowledge or drank during festivities to celebrate once more with their companions in battle. In Ustalav, cloaked ministers perform rituals with the blood of extraplanar entities to feed and strengthen their congregation against all manner of maladies and horrors. In Casmaron, divine entities coordinate with mortals to provide their blood as catalysts in wars against hidden threats.

Whatever the path, bloodragers have awakened magical powers to become dangerous forces of physical and magical might wherever there is blood to be found.

### Bloodrager 1st

You use the power of ingested blood to generate magical power and fuel you in combat, transforming you into a horrifyingly fearsome opponent. You must select Bloodrager Dedication as your 2nd-level class feat.

Prerequisites: You must be a [barbarian](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=57.md).

Bloodrager Adjustments: You are trained in [Athletics](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=36.md), [Medicine](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=42.md), and a number of additional skills equal to 2 plus your Intelligence modifier, instead of your normal starting skill proficiencies.

You must choose the bloodrager instinct for your barbarian instinct.

### Bloodrager Instinct

Drinking the potent magical blood of supernatural creatures has changed you and awakened magical power within you, along with a grim thirst.

Blood Rage (Instinct Ability): When raging, your physical attacks deal extra [persistent bleed damage](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=86.md) equal to half your additional damage from Rage. When you [Cast a Spell](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) that requires a spell attack roll while raging, apply the additional damage from Rage to that spell, even on a failure.

Specialization Ability: When using blood rage, increase the additional damage from Rage from 2 to 4. If you have greater weapon specialization, instead increase the damage from 4 to 8.

Raging Resistance: You resist slashing damage and persistent bleed damage, as well as damage dealt by the creature whose blood you last drank using Harvest Blood (see Bloodrager Dedication), regardless of the damage type.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Bloodrager Dedication Feat 2

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Class](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=397.md) [Dedication](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=572.md)
Source [War of Immortals pg. 60](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
Archetype [Bloodrager](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=283.md)
Class [Barbarian](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=57.md)
Prerequisites [Bloodrager](../Instinct/Instincts.aspx-ID=14.md)

---

Drinking powerful blood has awakened magical potential within you. You gain a spell repertoire with two cantrips of your choice, from either the arcane or [divine](../SpellLists/SpellLists.aspx-Tradition=2.md) spell list. You choose these cantrips from the common spells for your tradition or from other spells you have access to on that tradition’s spell list. At least one cantrip must require a spell attack roll. You’re trained in spell attack modifier and spell DC. Your key spellcasting attribute for these spells is Charisma. Spells in your repertoire gain the [rage](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=680.md) trait while you are raging, and when you [Cast a Spell](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) from your repertoire, you become [drained](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=68.md) 1 (or increase the value of your drained condition by 1); you can reduce the value of this condition only by Harvesting Blood.

You become trained in Arcana if you chose arcane cantrips or Religion if you chose divine cantrips. If you were already trained in this skill, you become trained in a skill of your choice. You also gain the Harvest Blood action.

### Harvest Blood [one-action]

Source [War of Immortals pg. 60](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
Requirements Your last action was a successful piercing or slashing melee Strike against a creature that is not immune to bleed

---

You consume the blood lingering upon your weapon. You reduce the value of your drained condition by 1, gain temporary Hit Points equal to your Constitution modifier, and gain a +1 circumstance bonus to saving throws against that creature's spells for 1 minute, or until you Harvest Blood from another creature.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Rising Blood Magic Feat 4

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md)

Archetype [Bloodrager](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=283.md)
Class [Barbarian](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=57.md)

---

The power within your blood grows. You gain the basic spellcasting benefits. Each time you gain a spell slot of a new rank from the bloodrager archetype, add a common spell of the appropriate tradition and rank to your repertoire. In addition, when you [Cast a Spell](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) from your repertoire and you are at least [drained](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=68.md) 1, increase the extra damage dealt by that spell from Rage by 1.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Split Shot [one-action] Feat 4

[Concentrate](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=561.md) [Sorcerer](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=824.md) [Spellshape](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=513.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 154](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetype [Bloodrager](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=283.md)

---

You fragment a ranged spell. If your next action is to [Cast a Spell](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) without a duration that requires an [attack](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=540.md) roll against a single target, you can choose a second target within range. You roll a single attack roll and compare the result to the AC of both targets. This counts as one attack for your multiple attack penalty. To the second target, the spell deals half the amount of damage it would normally deal and has no effects beyond the spell's initial damage (such as imposing conditions or penalties).

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Bespell Strikes [free-action] Feat 8

[Oracle](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=817.md) [Sorcerer](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=824.md) [Wizard](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=735.md)
Source [Player Core pg. 202](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md), [Player Core 2 pg. 139](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetypes [Battle Harbinger](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=304.md), [Bloodrager](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=283.md)
Frequency once per turn
Requirements Your most recent action was to cast a non-cantrip spell

---

You siphon spell energy into one weapon you’re wielding, or into one of your unarmed attacks, such as a fist. Until the end of your turn, the weapon or unarmed attack deals an extra 1d6 force damage and gains the associated trait (see below) if it didn’t have it already. If the spell dealt a different type of damage, the Strike deals this type of damage instead (or one type of your choice if the spell could deal multiple types of damage).

Oracle: Your strike gains the [divine](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=579.md) trait.

Sorcerer: Your strike gains the trait of your bloodline’s magical tradition.

Wizard: Your strike gains the [arcane](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=534.md) trait.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Spelldrinker [one-action] Feat 8

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md)

Archetype [Bloodrager](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=283.md)
Class [Barbarian](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=57.md)
Prerequisites Rising Blood Magic
Requirements Your last action was to Harvest Blood.

---

You temporarily add a 3rd-rank spell to your spell repertoire, as determined by the type of creature whose blood you just drank. If the creature has more than one of the listed traits, choose one and gain the appropriate spell. If the granted spell is not on your chosen tradition’s list, you cast the spell as though it was from your chosen tradition. The spell is removed from your spell repertoire after 24 hours, the next time you use Spelldrinker, or when you next make your [daily preparations](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2575.md), whichever comes first.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Hematocritical [free-action] Feat 10

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Fortune](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=612.md) [Rage](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=680.md)
Source [War of Immortals pg. 61](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
Archetype [Bloodrager](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=283.md)
Class [Barbarian](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=57.md)
Prerequisites Bloodrager Dedication
Requirements Your last action was a [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md) that critically hit a creature that isn’t immune to bleed damage.

---

You bathe in the arterial fluids of your enemy, drinking deep of their freed essence to empower your magic and increase your spellcasting efficacy. If your next action is to [Cast a Spell](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) that requires a spell attack roll, you roll the spell attack roll twice and use the better result; this is a [fortune](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=612.md) effect. Alternatively, if your next action is to cast a spell that requires the creature damaged by the triggering strike to attempt a save, they roll twice and take the lower result; this is a [misfortune](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=654.md) effect.
