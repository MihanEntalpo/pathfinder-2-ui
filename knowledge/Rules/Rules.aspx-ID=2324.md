# [Getting Knocked Out](./Rules.aspx-ID=2324.md)

Source [Player Core pg. 410](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md)

Creatures can't be reduced to fewer than 0 Hit Points. When most creatures reach 0 Hit Points, they die and are removed from play unless the attack was [nonlethal](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=661.md), in which case they're instead knocked out for a significant amount of time (usually 10 minutes or more). When [undead](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=722.md) and [constructs](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=563.md) reach 0 Hit Points, they're destroyed.

Player characters, their companions, and other significant characters and creatures don't automatically die when they reach 0 Hit Points. Instead, they are knocked out and are at risk of death. The GM might determine that villains, powerful monsters, special NPCs, and enemies with special abilities that are likely to bring them back to the fight (like ferocity, regeneration, or healing magic) can use these rules as well.

As a player character, when you're reduced to 0 Hit Points, you're knocked out with the following effects:

- Move your initiative position to directly before the turn in which you were reduced to 0 HP.
- Gain the [dying](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=69.md) 1 condition. If the effect that knocked you out was a critical success from the attacker or the result of your critical failure, you gain the dying 2 condition instead. If you have the [wounded](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=99.md) condition, increase your dying value by an amount equal to your wounded value. If the damage was dealt by a nonlethal attack or nonlethal effect, you don't gain the dying condition; you're instead [unconscious](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=95.md) with 0 Hit Points.
