A mysterious force granted you divine magic and a curse.

### Multiclass Oracle Characters

The oracle archetype is a great choice for characters who want to use powerful cursebound abilities that come at a cost. It's especially good for characters looking to add some high-risk, high-reward options.

- Martial oracles can use powerful cursebound abilities to access unique magical effects.
- Spellcaster oracles can further flex their diverse pool of options at the cost of a curse.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Oracle Dedication Feat 2

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Dedication](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=572.md) [Multiclass](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=659.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 180](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetype [Oracle](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=233.md)
Class [Oracle](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=61.md)
Prerequisites Charisma +2

---

Choose a mystery. You become trained in [Religion](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=46.md) and the mystery’s skill; if you were already trained, you become trained in a skill of your choice. You gain the curse associated with your mystery, which follows the normal rules for an oracular curse.

You cast spells like an oracle and gain the [Cast a Spell](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) activity. You gain a spell repertoire with two cantrips, either common divine cantrips or other divine cantrips you learn or discover. You’re trained in the spell attack modifier and spell DC statistics. Your key spellcasting attribute for oracle archetype spells is Charisma, and they are divine oracle spells.
