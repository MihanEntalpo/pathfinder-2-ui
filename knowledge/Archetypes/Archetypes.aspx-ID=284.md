Whether dead or simply missing, your patron has gone silent, and you can no longer hear its whisper. While other witches might find this silence disquieting and lose hope without direction, you embrace this new challenge. Without a governor, you can now realize the true potential of your magic by tapping into the connection you once shared with your absent patron. You are no longer bound by the your patron’s desires; you are free to forge your own path.

### Seneschal 1st

Your witch patron has gone mysteriously silent. You still have access to your abilities, and your familiar is still funneling your patron’s power to you. As you learn how to tap into that power, you influence your familiar to mold the power by itself, continuing to learn new spells and provide you with other witch class features.

Without your patron watching over your power, you gain more control but also miss important lessons. You must select Seneschal Witch Dedication as your 2nd level class feat.

Prerequisites: You must be a witch who has lost contact with your patron.

Adjustments: Instead of your patron’s lesson, you gain the manifest will hex cantrip and your familiar learns one common 1st-rank spell of your choice from your spell list.

# Additional Feats

Source [Player Core pg. 215](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md)
Some archetypes include a list of “Additional Feats” that appear in other sources. The list includes each feat’s level, which might be different than normal when gained from the archetype. You can take the feat as an archetype feat of that level, meaning it counts toward the number of feats required by the archetype’s dedication feat. When selected this way, a feat that normally has a class’s trait (such as the fighter trait) doesn’t have that class trait.

[Rare](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=683.md) [Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Class](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=397.md) [Dedication](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=572.md)

As you work to understand the connection with your missing patron, it’s increasingly easy to form bonds with those around you. Raw magical energy spills easily through the connection but, without a patron’s control, it sometimes resists your demands for it to form proper spells.

You gain the [Witch’s Charge](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=5003.md) feat, but can’t choose your [familiar](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=5024.md) or another creature with the [minion](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=653.md) trait as a charge. When you [cast](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2734.md) or [Sustain](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2317.md) manifest will, as long as your charge is within 30 feet, you can choose to center the spell’s [emanation](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2387.md) on your charge instead of yourself.

Gathering up threads of your old connection, you can temporarily disguise yourself as your missing patron. You gain [illusory disguise](../Spell/Spells.aspx-ID=1568.md) as an innate spell of your patron’s tradition, automatically heightened to the rank of your witch cantrips, but you can use it only to disguise yourself as the [humanoid](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=628.md) representation of your patron. Detection spells of lower rank than this illusory disguise give your patron’s information rather than your own.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Patron Reborn Feat 20

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md)
Source [War of Immortals pg. 63](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
Archetype [Seneschal](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=284.md)
Class [Witch](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=38.md)
Prerequisites Seneschal Witch Dedication

---

You have fully realized the ability to serve as a patron.

Your newfound abilities enhance your senses, rolling back the veil of a realm hidden to most mortals where great beings vie for unimaginable influence over vast worlds. You gain [darkvision](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2410.md), constant [truesight](../Spell/Spells.aspx-ID=1727.md), and the ability to see into all other transitive planes that overlap the one you are in.

You can be a patron for other witches. Choose a single type of patron you represent, such as [The Resentment](../Patrons/Patrons.aspx-ID=14.md) or [Starless Shadow](../Patrons/Patrons.aspx-ID=17.md). An allied witch you can observe who has that patron counts as and gains the benefits of your [Witch’s Charge](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=5003.md) feat, even if you did not select them as your charge during your [daily preparations](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2575.md).

You learn the [wish](../Ritual/Rituals.aspx-ID=125.md) ritual and can impart that knowledge to your charges. Once per month, when one of your charges performs the wish ritual, you can roll the skill check in their place.
