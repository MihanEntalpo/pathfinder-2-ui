Bulk &mdash;

You’ve linked your lifespan to your bound dragon. Your natural physical aging ceases and you gain the Draconic Vitality action, but your soul grows withered and scabrous by the weight of a dragon’s experience with time.

Once per week, from any distance, the dragon can use a free action, which has the [concentrate](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=561.md) trait, to become [quickened](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=89.md) for 1 minute. They can use the extra action to [Stride](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2305.md), [Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306.md), or to contribute an action to casting a spell. When the dragon uses this ability, you age 10 years and become [drained](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=68.md) 3 for 1 hour.

Activate—Draconic Vitality [one-action] ([concentrate](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=561.md)); Frequency once per day; Effect Reduce your [doomed](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=67.md) value by 2. For 1 hour, you gain a +2 status bonus to saves against [poisons](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=669.md) and diseases, and a status bonus equal to your level to Hit Points you regain from spells with the same tradition as your bound dragon.

---

Oathbreaker’s Calamity Should either party break the oath, you begin aging at an accelerated rate. Every 1d6 days, you must succeed at a [flat DC 14 check](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=333.md) or age 10 years and become [drained](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=68.md) 3. In turn, the dragon loses years of experience, reducing its age 100 years every time you fail the flat check. If the dragon is de-aged past the time they made the pact, the pact remains, but the dragon loses all memories of you and the pact.
