# Coralclaw

With the physique of a giant lobster and the artistic sensibilities of a hermit crab, a coralclaw relies on ambush to catch prey. As their name suggests, they adorn their shells by secreting an adhesive and attaching broken corals to blend in with their local reefs. Older coralclaws often foster barnacles, anemones, and even living coral on their back to enhance the disguise. Once properly adorned, the coralclaw finds a hollow to hide within and wait for prey.

### Coralclaw Caches

When a coralclaw molts, it leaves behind its coral-covered shell. These husks provide temporary homes for various fish, and they warn divers that an ambush predator dwells in the area. In addition, these shells are often studded with not just valuable corals and shells, but coins, glassware dredged from forgotten shipwrecks, and even the occasional magical item!
