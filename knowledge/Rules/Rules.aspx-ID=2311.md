# [Nonlethal Attacks](./Rules.aspx-ID=2311.md)

Source [Player Core pg. 407](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md)

You can make a nonlethal attack to knock someone out instead of killing them (see [Knocked Out](./Rules.aspx-ID=2324.md) and [Dying](./Rules.aspx-ID=2325.md)). Weapons with the [nonlethal](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=661.md) trait (including [fists](../Weapon/Weapons.aspx-ID=356.md)) do this automatically. You take a –2 circumstance penalty to the attack roll when you make a nonlethal attack using a weapon or unarmed attack that doesn’t have the nonlethal trait. You also take this penalty when making a lethal attack using a nonlethal weapon. Spells and other effects with the nonlethal trait that reduce a creature to 0 Hit Points knock the creature out instead of killing them.
