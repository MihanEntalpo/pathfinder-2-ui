# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Upion and Warrik (The Shieldbrothers)](./Deities.aspx-ID=705.md)

Source [Pathfinder #216: The Acropolis Pyre pg. 78](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=271.md), [Myth-Speakers Players Guide pg. 11](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=270.md)
When cyclopes invited Upion to receive his myth-speaking, he politely declined, unwilling to suffer the accompanying longevity if it meant outliving his lover, Warrik, by decades or even centuries. Instead, the pair continued their military careers, perishing together in the Battle of Tesikon Heights. Yet the two revived moments later, flushed with mythic power that they used to turn the tide of battle. Ever since, Upion and Warrik have acted as a single hero-god, their divine abilities fading when they're separated. The two encourage camaraderie, steadfastness, and good cheer.
