# Ikons

Source [War of Immortals pg. 43](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
As an exemplar, you select three ikons at first level. Typically, you will want to ensure at least one of your ikons is a weapon. Body ikons accentuate a physical trait of your body and so can’t be stolen, disarmed, dispelled, or otherwise taken away. Weapon and worn ikons are tied to items of power. When you select one, you gain a non- magical, level-0 item of your choice that matches its usage entry. Providence ensures you come across these items; you might be traveling along a path to find a spear in a tree that only you can dislodge, or you might awaken holding a gleaming sash you saw in your dreams.

Your ikons can be etched with runes, upgraded, or otherwise modified as normal for items of their type. A body or worn ikon can have runes etched on it to apply to your unarmed attacks as though it were [handwraps of mighty blows](../Equipment/Equipment.aspx-ID=3086.md), though only one of your ikons can have these runes and no ikon can have both these and armor or weapon runes.

If you acquire a new item the ikon’s usage could apply to, you can switch your ikon to the new item by spending 1 day of downtime with the new ikon as you saturate the object with your divine energy. You can use this process to make an existing magic item, like a [cloak of illusions](../Equipment/Equipment.aspx-ID=3069.md) or a [searing blade](../Equipment/Equipment.aspx-ID=2870.md), into your ikon. If the item wasn’t already a divine item, it becomes one for as long as it is your ikon, removing the arcane, occult, primal, or magical trait from the item and adding the divine trait. Artifacts, intelligent items, and other similarly powerful objects might resist your attempts to exert your divinity over them, with unpredictable results determined by the GM.

# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Bands Of Imprisonment Worn Ikon

[Ikon](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=840.md)
Source [War of Immortals pg. 43](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
Usage worn anklets, bracers, or circlet (often a headband)

---

These weighted bands don’t enhance your power—rather, they keep your strength in check, honing your discipline.

## Immanence

The bands of imprisonment tighten, keeping your mind sharp. You gain a +1 status bonus to Will saving throws and resistance to [mental](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=647.md) damage equal to half your level.

## Transcendence — Break Free [two-actions]

[Transcendence](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=842.md)
You can attempt to [Escape](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2296.md) with a +2 status bonus on your check, then [Stride](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2305.md) up to twice your Speed in a straight line, and finally make a melee Strike. If you don’t need to Escape or you can’t move or choose not to, you still take the other actions listed.

# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Horn Of Plenty Worn Ikon

[Extradimensional](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=596.md) [Ikon](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=840.md)
Source [War of Immortals pg. 44](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=232.md)
Usage a wallet, bag, or similar container of 1 Bulk you can wear on your body

---

Whether a bag, gourd, wallet, cornucopia, or similar food receptacle, this ikon recalls the harvest and hearth. The receptacle can store up to 1 Bulk of potions and elixirs, but no other items. The receptacle can’t be opened except by the ikon’s immanence and transcendence abilities.

Each day during your [daily preparations](../Rules/Rules.aspx-ID=2575.md), the ikon produces one temporary elixir of life. You can choose to have it make a different [elixir](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=589.md) or [potion](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=672.md) you know the formula for. The level of any elixir or potion created by the horn must be your level or lower. The number of elixirs the horn creates increases to two at 8th level and three at 16th level, and you can choose each item individually. These temporary items vanish the next time you make your daily preparations, and any remaining effects of the temporary items end. A temporary elixir or potion has no value.

## Immanence

The horn of plenty shimmers, allowing access to the stored consumables inside. You can [Interact](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2297.md) to draw a consumable and drink it in a single action while your divine spark rests within the horn. Other creatures can’t access the contents unless you allow them to.

## Transcendence — Feed the Masses [one-action]

[Transcendence](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=842.md)
The horn of plenty allows you to transfer the effects of potions and elixirs to your allies. You Interact to draw a consumable from the horn and then Interact to drink it. Rather than nourishing yourself, the item’s effects are transferred to a willing ally within 60 feet, as if they had consumed it themself. If the consumable restores Hit Points, you can choose to divide the amount it restores between you and the ally freely (after rolling dice to determine the amount).
