Dark Archives (Remastered)

[Psychic Duels](./Rules.aspx-ID=3514.md)

Source [Dark Archives (Remastered) pg. 202](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=293.md)

Changing location is usually easy within a psychic duel, as a participant can move at the speed of thought. It typically takes only a single action to move to any location the participant wants and range of attacks and spells rarely matter much. You can usually run a psychic duel without a grid. Functions of positioning and movement still matter, but have a more flexible appearance. For instance, a participant can [Hide](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2404.md), but this likely means shaping the imaginary environment around them to create a visual screen, glowing phenomenon, or even psychic static. Likewise, spells that [immobilize](../Condition/Conditions.aspx-ID=81.md) or [grab](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2376.md) a creature can prevent the easy movement detailed above.
