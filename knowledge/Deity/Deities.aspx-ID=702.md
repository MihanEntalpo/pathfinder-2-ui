# [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)[Drokalion (The Pridefather)](./Deities.aspx-ID=702.md)

Source [Pathfinder #216: The Acropolis Pyre pg. 77](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=271.md), [Myth-Speakers Players Guide pg. 11](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=270.md)
Traditionally, hero-gods undergo the myth-speaking ritual and perform some great feat to obtain divinity. Not so for Drokalion, a male lion that barely seems aware of his mythic powers or the magic he grants. His worshippers consider this a feature, not a flaw; they laud and replicate Drokalion's primal strength, bravery, and rustic lifestyle. While none of them try to be eaten by their patron, the fate is one they would celebrate.

Category Iblydos Hero-Gods
Edicts Embody a lion's hunting strategies, savor meat, be chased or eaten by Drokalion
Anathema Back down from a fair challenge, cower to avoid harm, ruin Drokalion's hunt
Areas of Concern Being a lion
Religious Symbol roaring lion
Sacred Animal lion
Sacred Color(s) brown, yellow
