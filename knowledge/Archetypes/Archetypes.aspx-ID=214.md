### Multiclass Fighter Characters

The fighter archetype grants access to great feats for characters focusing on any combat style, and it’s particularly helpful for characters looking to diversify their offensive abilities or focus on more powerful weapons.

- [Bard](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=32.md) fighters lead warriors from the front, using their martial skills to stay in the thick of combat where their audience is.
- [Cleric](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=33.md) fighters focus on a style of combat exemplified by their deity. A shield-using warpriest benefits from the best of both worlds in shield feats.
- [Ranger](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=36.md) fighters can either specialize in archery or use their fighter feats to pick up an unusual combat style combination.
- [Rogue](../Class/Classes.aspx-ID=37.md) fighters can make good use of fighter’s free hand, two-weapon fighting, or archery feats. Making multiple [Strikes](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2306&Redirected=1.md) can serve them well.
- Spellcaster fighters benefit greatly from the additional weapon proficiencies, and fighter is a solid choice for any spellcaster of a particularly martial bent.

### [Reactive Strike](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2256.md) [reaction]

Source [Player Core pg. 138](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md)
Trigger A creature within your reach uses a [manipulate](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=645&Redirected=1.md) action or a [move](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=658&Redirected=1.md) action, makes a ranged attack, or leaves a square during a move action it's using.

---

You lash out at a foe that leaves an opening. Make a melee Strike against the triggering creature. If your attack is a critical hit and the trigger was a [manipulate](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=645&Redirected=1.md) action, you disrupt that action. This Strike doesn't count toward your multiple attack penalty, and your multiple attack penalty doesn't apply to this Strike.
