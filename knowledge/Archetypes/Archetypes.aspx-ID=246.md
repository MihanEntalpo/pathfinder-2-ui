# Additional Feats

Source [Player Core pg. 215](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=216.md)
Some archetypes include a list of “Additional Feats” that appear in other sources. The list includes each feat’s level, which might be different than normal when gained from the archetype. You can take the feat as an archetype feat of that level, meaning it counts toward the number of feats required by the archetype’s dedication feat. When selected this way, a feat that normally has a class’s trait (such as the fighter trait) doesn’t have that class trait.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Dandy Dedication Feat 2

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Dedication](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=572.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 195](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetype [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md)
Prerequisites trained in [Diplomacy](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=39.md)

---

You are a consummate student of dignity, etiquette, and fashion. You become trained in [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md) and [Society](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=47.md); if you were already trained in either, you become an expert in it instead. You gain the Influence Rumor downtime activity.

### Influence Rumor

Source [Player Core 2 pg. 195](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)

---

([downtime](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=580.md)) You spend at least 1 day of downtime attempting to manipulate the course, tone, or content of a rumor to your benefit. You need to succeed at a Diplomacy check to shift the rumor as you intend. The difficulty is determined by the GM based on the size of the community, the relative perceptiveness of the inhabitants, and the agency of other rumormongers, but it typically is no lower than DC 15 for a small village, DC 20 for a town, DC 30 for a city, or DC 40 for a metropolis.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Distracting Flattery [reaction] Feat 4

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Skill](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=695.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 195](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetypes [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md), [Venture-Gossip](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=311.md)
Prerequisites Dandy Dedication; expert in [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md)
Trigger You observe a target’s attitude toward yourself or your allies decrease as a result of an ally’s behavior.

---

You know how to maintain a good impression and manage your image, even while keeping uncouth company. Make a [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md) check against the target’s Will DC. Regardless of your result, the target is temporarily immune to your Distracting Flattery for 10 minutes.

Success The target’s attitude doesn’t decrease as a result of
your ally’s social blunder.
Failure The target’s attitude decreases as normal.
Critical Failure Your attempt makes matters worse, decreasing
the target’s attitude toward you by one step, in addition to
any changes from the behavior that triggered this reaction.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Gossip Lore Feat 4

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 195](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetypes [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md), [Prophet of Kalistrade](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=315.md), [Venture-Gossip](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=311.md)
Prerequisites Dandy Dedication

---

Your time gossiping in fashionable salons keeps you informed on every topic. You are trained in Gossip Lore, a special Lore skill that can be used only to Recall Knowledge, but on any topic. If you fail a check to Recall Knowledge with Gossip Lore, you get the effects of the [Dubious Knowledge](../Feat/Feats.aspx-ID=5142.md) skill feat. If you have legendary proficiency in [Society](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=47.md), you gain expert proficiency in Gossip Lore, but you can’t increase your proficiency rank in Gossip Lore by any other means.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Tut-Tut [free-action] Feat 4

### Legacy Content

Uncommon Archetype Emotion Fear Mental Press
Source Firebrands pg. 79
Archetypes [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md), Duelist
Prerequisites Duelist Dedication, or Dandy Dedication
Access Members of the Firebrands at the rank of second mark or higher have access to this feat.
Trigger You hit a creature with a melee attack roll.

---

The Sapphire Butterfly is not one for violence when simply intimidating a foe will do. From that vigilante's techniques, you've learned how to pull a deadly blow at the last moment, threatening your target instead of striking them. Rather than dealing damage with the triggering Strike, attempt an Intimidation check with a +2 circumstance bonus to Demoralize all enemies within 30 feet who can see you. If you critically succeed at your check to Demoralize the target of your Strike, their attitude toward you improves by one step while they also become frightened 2.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Fabricated Connections Feat 7

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Skill](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=695.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 195](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetypes [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md), [Venture-Gossip](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=311.md)
Prerequisites Dandy Dedication; master in [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md)

---

You can lie about having taken an action so convincingly that you acquire all the tangible benefits of having successfully completed it. You can roll [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md) in place of a different skill when attempting to Earn Income, [Make an Impression](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2392.md), [Request](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2393.md), or Subsist. You can use Fabricated Connections to Make an Impression or Request once per day, and you can also use it once per week to Earn Income or Subsist.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Party Crasher Feat 7

[Archetype](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=535.md) [Skill](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=695.md)
Source [Player Core 2 pg. 195](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=227.md)
Archetypes [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md), [Venture-Gossip](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=311.md)
Prerequisites Dandy Dedication; master in [Society](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=47.md)

---

You attend all the fashionable society parties, no matter how exclusive they might be. Any time you encounter a social event you would ordinarily be denied access to, such as a coronation, royal gala, or other society function, you can spend 1d4 hours to secure entry without the need for a skill check. You find invitations, invitees looking for fashionable dates, temporary jobs with the caterers, event staff willing to look the other way, or some other mode of access for yourself and your allies. This ability doesn’t apply to secret events or other small private gatherings with no staff, dates, or outsiders involved.

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Operatic Adventurer Feat 12

## [PFS.aspx.htm](../PFS/PFS.aspx.md)Play to the Crowd Feat 12

### This Feat may contain spoilers from the Curtain Call Adventure Path

Uncommon Archetype Skill
Source [Pathfinder #204: Stage Fright pg. 82](../Source/Sources.aspx-ID=245.md)
Archetype [Dandy](./Archetypes.aspx-ID=246.md)
Prerequisites Dandy Dedication; master in [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md)

---

You have a knack for knowing just how to manipulate groups by saying exactly what needs to be said. When you Lie in a public area in front of a crowd, you can gauge the reactions of the crowd as you speak to know how your [Lie](../Action/Actions.aspx-ID=2389.md) is working, and Lie loses its [secret](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=690.md) trait. You gain the Reactive Falsehood reaction.

Reactive Falsehood [reaction] ([fortune](../Trait/Traits.aspx-ID=612.md)) Frequency once per hour; Trigger You fail to Lie to a creature in public; Effect As your target sees through your lie, you spin your falsehood in a way that utilizes the crowd’s reaction to bolster believability. Re-roll your [Deception](../Skill/Skills.aspx-ID=38.md) check to Lie with a +2 status bonus and use this check’s result as the actual result to Lie.
